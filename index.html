I'm a first-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Yale University, advised by Prof. Mark Gerstein. Previously, I got my master's at Yale CS as well. I've interned at Tencent AI Lab, Microsoft Research Asia, and Sinovation Ventures.

My research lies in the foundations of biomedical data science with applications in machine learning, natural language processing, and bioinformatics. My goal is to use language models to provide interesting biological insights.

As steps towards this goal, I work on:
(a) Foundation models of biomedical data: representation and integration of data by large language models.
(b) Store, model, and reason about scientific knowledge: writing scientific literature and bioinformatics code.
(c) Deciphering the principle of genetics and genomics: regulatory genomics under the single-cell and multi-omics context.

I'm affiliated with Gerstein Lab (Yale Computational Biology and Bioinformatics). Follow @Gerstein Lab Yale University
Current projects: DNA language modeling and NLG evaluation.
