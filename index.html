I'm a second-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Yale University, advised by Prof. Mark Gerstein. Previously, I got my master's from Yale CS as well, advised by Prof. Dragomir Radev. My research lies in the intersection of large language models (LLMs) and applications in biology.

  • AI for biology: I aim to design novel and useful biomolecules with LLMs, like In-Context Learning and Chain-of-Thought Prompting.
  • Large Language Models: I study how LLMs can produce extremely complex and intelligent behaviors via multi-agent collaboration and the exploration of tool use strategies.
  • Code Generation: I aim to make programming more communicative, by creating models, methods, and datasets for producing code from language.
My research is supported by my advisor's Amazon Research Award.
